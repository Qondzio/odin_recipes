Spaghetti

Description:

This Classic Spaghetti Recipe is a simple yet flavorful dish featuring perfectly cooked pasta coated in a rich, homemade marinara sauce. The sauce is made by sautéing onions and garlic in olive oil, then simmering crushed tomatoes with aromatic herbs like oregano and basil. A hint of red pepper flakes adds subtle heat, while a touch of sugar balances the acidity of the tomatoes.

Once the sauce thickens, it's tossed with al dente spaghetti, ensuring every strand is coated in delicious flavor. To finish, a generous sprinkle of Parmesan cheese and fresh basil enhances the dish, making it both comforting and satisfying. Whether served as a quick weeknight meal or a cozy dinner, this spaghetti recipe is a timeless classic that never fails to impress.

Ingredients:

  • 350 g spaghetti
  • 15 g salt (for boiling water)
  • 15 ml olive oil (optional)
  • 30 ml olive oil
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 800 g canned crushed tomatoes
  • 2 g black pepper
  • 3 g dried oregano
  • 2 g red pepper flakes (optional, for spice)
  • 15 g fresh basil, chopped (or 3 g dried basil)

Steps:

  1. Cook the spaghetti: Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Add salt. Cook the spaghetti according to package instructions until al dente. Drain and toss with a little olive oil to prevent sticking. Set aside.
  2. Prepare the marinara sauce: Heat olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Sauté onions until soft (about 3-4 minutes), then add garlic and cook for 1 more minute. Add crushed tomatoes, salt, pepper, oregano, red pepper flakes, and sugar. Stir well. Simmer for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally. Stir in fresh basil and turn off the heat.
  3. Combine and serve: Toss the cooked spaghetti with the marinara sauce. Plate and top with Parmesan cheese and fresh basil.
